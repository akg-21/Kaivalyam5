Our Mission

Guiding principles that shape our work

Kaivalyam is dedicated to fostering profound transformation for neurodivergent children. Our journey centers on learning and healing, with a focus on true inclusion—embracing individuals for who they are while accepting ourselves in the process.

We create a space of mutual support built on honesty and trust, driven by the strong intention to grow both personally and in our relationships with neurodivergent individuals.

BHAAVAM

A specialized dance school for neurodivergent individuals, creating a sanctuary where they can discover their talents and embark on a journey toward life's purpose, experiencing peace and happiness through music and movement.

Methodology

Our Approach

Holistic, neurodiversity-affirming principles

Neurodiversity-Affirming

We recognize neurological differences as natural variations in the human experience. Our programs emphasize strengths while providing support for challenges.

Movement as Therapy

Physical movement is key to neurological development and sensory integration. Our therapeutic movement programs help children develop body awareness and coordination.

Inclusive Community

We foster a sense of belonging and acceptance where children and families connect with others who understand their experiences and challenges.

Individualized Support

Every child's needs are unique. We tailor our approaches to each child's specific profile of strengths and challenges, adjusting as they grow.

Meet Our Founder

Expertise guided by compassion and experience

Lakshmi Sudhakaran

Founder & Lead Instructor, Kaivalyam

MPhil (Psychology), PGDSE, BPT, SI Certified Therapist

Lakshmi Sudhakaran is an avid follower of Anthroposophy, a physiotherapist with a master's degree in Psychology. Her exceptional ability to understand and meet the learning needs of children and adults with special needs has established her as one of the most accomplished therapists and educators in her field.

Her career began in 2002 in Mumbai, where she received intensive training in Sensory Integration and NDT. Upon returning to Chennai, she expanded her involvement to include administrative aspects of therapy, assessment and oversight of children's day-to-day functioning, personalized therapy and training programs, and mentoring and supporting parents and families.

Currently, she serves as a consultant at various schools for special children in Chennai, including Vasantha Memorial Trust, Aikya Foundation, and Alpha To Omega Learning Centre.
