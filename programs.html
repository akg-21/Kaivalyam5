Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our training program

Who is this course for?

This course is designed for therapists, educators, healthcare professionals, and parents who want to gain a deeper understanding of sensory processing challenges and effective therapeutic interventions. It's ideal for those working with neurodivergent children or those looking to expand their professional skill set.

What prerequisites are required?

No specific prerequisites are required, though a background in education, psychology, therapy, healthcare, or related fields is beneficial. The course is structured to accommodate both beginners and experienced professionals.

How is the course structured?

The course combines online modules, live webinars, case studies, and hands-on practice. You'll learn at your own pace through the modules, participate in scheduled live sessions, and have the opportunity for in-person training. Each component builds upon the others to provide a comprehensive learning experience.

What is the time commitment?

The course runs for 8 weeks, with approximately 5-7 hours of commitment per week. This includes self-paced learning, live sessions, and practice time. The weekend in-person training is an additional 2 days (optional but highly recommended).

Is certification provided?

Yes, upon successful completion of the course requirements, you will receive a professional certification. This certification is recognized by various professional organizations and can count toward continuing education units (CEUs).

Meet Your Instructor

Guidance from experienced professionals

Lakshmi Sudhakaran

Founder & Lead Instructor, Kaivalyam

MPhil (Psychology), PGDSE, BPT, SI Certified Therapist

Lakshmi Sudhakaran is an avid follower of Anthroposophy, a physiotherapist with a master's degree in Psychology. Her exceptional ability to understand and meet the learning needs of children and adults with special needs has established her as one of the most accomplished therapists and educators in her field.

Her career began in 2002 in Mumbai, where she received intensive training in Sensory Integration and NDT. Upon returning to Chennai, she expanded her involvement to include administrative aspects of therapy, assessment and oversight of children's day-to-day functioning, personalized therapy and training programs, and mentoring and supporting parents and families.

Currently, she serves as a consultant at various schools for special children in Chennai, including Vasantha Memorial Trust, Aikya Foundation, and Alpha To Omega Learning Centre.
